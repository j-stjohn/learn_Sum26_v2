# Internet Routing Architectures
## Chapter 1 — Evolution of the Internet

### Core Concept
- The Internet evolved from a small research network into a global network of interconnected organizations.
- No single organization owns the Internet.
- The Internet operates through cooperation between independent networks.
- Routing exists to move data between these networks.

## Early Networking

### Standalone Computers
- Early computers operated independently.
- Data transfer required physical media.
- Examples:
	- Punch cards
	- Magnetic tape
	- Floppy disks

### Need for Networking
- Organizations wanted to:
	- Share resources
	- Exchange data
	- Communicate remotely
- Networks emerged to connect computers together.

## ARPANET

### Definition
- ARPANET was the predecessor to the modern Internet.
- Funded by the U.S. Department of Defense.

### Goals
- Enable communication between research institutions.
- Create resilient communications infrastructure.
- Allow resource sharing between computers.

### Significance
- Demonstrated large-scale packet-switched networking.
- Established many concepts still used today.

## Packet Switching

### Traditional Telephone Networks
- Circuit-switched.
- Dedicated communication path established before communication begins.

### Packet Switching
- Data is divided into small packets.
- Packets travel independently through the network.
- Multiple conversations share the same infrastructure.

### Advantages
- Efficient use of bandwidth.
- Improved reliability.
- Better scalability.

## TCP/IP

### Purpose
- Provides a common language for network communication.

### Transmission Control Protocol (TCP)
Responsibilities:
- Reliable delivery
- Error checking
- Packet ordering
- Retransmission

### Internet Protocol (IP)
Responsibilities:
- Addressing
- Routing packets between networks

### Importance
- Allowed different networks to communicate.
- Became the foundation of the Internet.

## Growth of the Internet

### Initial Environment
- Small number of research institutions.
- Limited number of connected systems.

### Expansion
- Universities joined.
- Government agencies joined.
- Commercial organizations joined.

### Result
- Rapid increase in:
	- Users
	- Networks
	- Routing complexity

## Autonomous Networks

### Problem
- As the Internet grew, centralized control became impractical.

### Solution
- Independent organizations manage their own networks.
- Networks cooperate to exchange traffic.

### Examples
- Universities
- ISPs
- Government agencies
- Corporations

## Routing

### Definition
- The process of selecting a path for data to travel through a network.

### Router
- A device that forwards packets between networks.

### Routing Goals
- Reach destination successfully.
- Choose efficient paths.
- Adapt to network failures.

## The Internet as a Network of Networks

### Key Idea
- The Internet is not one network.
- The Internet is many interconnected networks.

### Characteristics
- Decentralized
- Distributed
- Cooperative

### Benefits
- Scalability
- Reliability
- Flexibility

## Challenges Introduced by Growth

### Scale
- Increasing number of networks.

### Policy
- Organizations have different business goals.

### Administration
- No single authority controls all routing decisions.

### Complexity
- Routing decisions become more difficult as connectivity expands.

## Why Routing Matters

### Without Routing
- Packets cannot move between networks.
- Communication remains local.

### With Routing
- Global communication becomes possible.
- Independent networks can exchange traffic.
- The Internet functions as a unified system.

## Key Ideas

### The Internet Was Not Designed All At Once
- It evolved gradually.
- New technologies were added over time.

### Packet Switching Changed Networking
- Shared infrastructure replaced dedicated circuits.

### TCP/IP Unified Networks
- Different systems could communicate using common protocols.

### Growth Created Routing Challenges
- More networks required better routing methods.

### The Internet Is a Network of Networks
- Independent organizations cooperate to provide connectivity.
